# Sales & Products EDA — Key Insights

## 1. Data Overview
- The dataset covers sales transactions across multiple products, regions, store types, salespeople, and payment methods.
- Time coverage spans from 2023 to 2025, allowing both seasonal and year-over-year analysis.

---

## 2. Revenue Contribution & Concentration

### Salesperson Contribution
- Revenue contribution is relatively balanced across salespeople.
- Top contributors:
  - Bob (18.19%)
  - Alice (17.95%)
  - Frank (16.32%)
- No single salesperson dominates revenue, indicating low dependency risk.

### Store Performance
- Revenue is evenly distributed across store types:
  - Store D leads with 27.52%
  - Other stores contribute between 23%–25%
- This suggests consistent performance across stores rather than reliance on a single outlet.

### Regional Distribution
- Revenue is well spread geographically:
  - North region leads (22.10%)
  - South is lowest (18.90%)
- Regional sales are balanced, reducing geographic concentration risk.

---

## 3. Product Performance

### Volume vs Revenue
- Tablets, Laptops, and Printers generate the highest total revenue.
- Phones have the lowest revenue despite moderate sales volume.

### Pricing Insight (Weighted Average)
- High average unit prices:
  - Chair (~319.7)
  - Monitor (~317.3)
- Lower-priced, high-volume products:
  - Phone (~252.4)

### Product Segmentation
- Most products fall into the **Balanced** category.
- Monitors are classified as **Low Volume / High Revenue**, indicating premium pricing and margin potential.

---

## 4. Sales Efficiency

### Salesperson Efficiency
- Bob generates the highest total revenue and highest revenue per unit.
- Alice and Eva show strong average order values, suggesting higher-value transactions.
- Efficiency varies more in pricing quality than in quantity sold.

---

## 5. Customer & Payment Behavior

### Customer Type
- Retail and Wholesale customers contribute almost equally to revenue.
- Revenue streams are diversified across customer types.

### Payment Methods
- Online and Cash payments generate the highest revenue.
- Debit Card contributes the least, suggesting possible friction or preference differences.

---

## 6. Returns Analysis
- Carlos has the highest number of returns, followed by Bob and Alice.
- This may indicate higher sales volume, product mix issues, or post-sale quality concerns.

---

## 7. Time-Based Performance

### Monthly Trends
- Average monthly sales: 145,999.75
- Worst months:
  - Oct 2023
  - May 2023
  - Dec 2023
- Best months:
  - Mar 2023
  - Mar 2025
  - Oct 2024
- Clear seasonality is present.

### Yearly Performance
- 2024 showed positive growth (+4.36%) compared to 2023.
- 2025 shows a sharp decline (-48.63%), likely due to partial-year data.

---

## 8. Key Takeaway
Sales performance is balanced across people, regions, and stores, with clear seasonality and identifiable premium products. Growth peaked in 2024, while 2025 performance should be evaluated cautiously due to incomplete data.
